Sourdough

Maintaining a healthy culture

Your sourdough culture needs to be fed every day to remain healthy and alive, alternatively you can keep it in the fridge and feed weekly. Feeding instructions:

  1. Remove all but one tablespoon of culture, discard or use for baking.
  2. Mix 50g bread flour, 60g water and remaining tablespoon of culture.
  3. Store in an airtight container, with plenty of headroom for expansion.
  4. Store container in a cool place.

Use filtered water, or leave tap water out for an hour before using to dissipate chlorine.

Alternatively you may use whole rye flour instead of bread flour, in which case reduce water to 50g.

A healthy culture will show bubbles after 12 hours.

Bread Recipe

Prepare sourdough

  1. Add one tablespoon of culture to 70g bread flour and 85g of water
  2. Mix until all incorporated.
  3. Cover with a damp teatowel
  4. Leave for 14-16 hours

Loaf

  1. Add together:
    • 390g bread flour
    • 45g whole rye or whole wheat flour
    • 210g water
    • 8g/half tablespoon salt
    • all sourdough mixture
  2. Mix well and knead on a floured surface until forms smooth round ball
  3. Prove for 2.5 hours
  4. Press out all gas and form into loaf shape
  5. Prove for 2-2.5 hours
  6. Deeply slash the top of the loaf
  7. Bake in 220°C oven for 40-45 minutes
